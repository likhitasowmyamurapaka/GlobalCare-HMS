Pediatrics

Pediatrics is a medical specialty focusing on the healthcare of newborns, children, and adolescents. Early identification and treatment of health issues are crucial to supporting the child’s growth and long-term well-being. A Pediatrician specializes in diagnosing, preventing, and managing childhood diseases, infections, nutritional disorders, developmental issues, and behavioral conditions.

Our Pediatric Care Unit includes General Pediatrics, Neonatal Intensive Care, Pediatric Emergency Care, Pediatric Surgery, Vaccination Programs, Growth & Development Monitoring, and Management of Chronic Childhood Illnesses. We ensure gentle, effective, and compassionate care for all pediatric conditions.

Symptoms

Symptoms in children vary depending on age, health condition, and infection type. Infants often show different signs compared to older children. Parents should observe symptoms closely as children may not always express what they feel.

  • Fever
  • Cold, cough, and throat pain
  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Stomach pain
  • Reduced appetite
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Breathing difficulty
  • Skin rashes
  • Persistent crying in infants
  • Swelling in the body
  • Delayed growth or developmental milestones
  • Frequent infections
  • Dehydration signs (dry lips, no tears while crying)
