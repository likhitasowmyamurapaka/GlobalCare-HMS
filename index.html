Health Awareness & Emergency Tips

CPR Basics

Learn how to respond to cardiac arrest using the basic steps of Hands-Only CPR until help arrives.

Hands-only CPR can save lives. Follow the correct rhythm (100–120 compressions per minute), push hard and fast in the center of the chest, and do not stop until help arrives.

Read More

First Aid During Accidents

Know the essential first-aid actions to take during cuts, burns, fractures, or sudden injuries.

Immediate first aid can prevent complications during cuts, burns, sprains, or injuries. Keep the person calm, stop bleeding, and avoid moving them unnecessarily until help arrives.

Read More

When to Call Emergency

Identify the warning signs that require immediate emergency medical assistance.

Call emergency services when someone has severe bleeding, chest pain, breathing difficulty, or unconsciousness. Act quickly, stay on the line with responders, and follow instructions until medical help reaches you.
